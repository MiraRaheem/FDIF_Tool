Excellent question, Reham 💪

Always run your FastAPI (and anything Python-related for this project) **inside your virtual environment** — i.e. when your PowerShell prompt shows `(.venv)` at the beginning.

---

### 💡 Why:

The virtual environment ensures:

* You’re using **the right Python interpreter** for your project.
* All installed packages (FastAPI, Uvicorn, rdflib, etc.) are available.
* You don’t accidentally use or break system-wide packages.

---

### ✅ So your prompt should look like:

```
(.venv) PS D:\Narrate\fdif_poc>
```

Then run:

```powershell
uvicorn app.main:app --reload
```

If your prompt **doesn’t** have `(.venv)` (meaning the venv is not active), you can activate it anytime with:

```powershell
.\.venv\Scripts\Activate.ps1
```

You’ll instantly see:

```
(.venv) PS D:\Narrate\fdif_poc>
```

Then you’re good to run Uvicorn.

---

Once it’s running, open:

* [http://127.0.0.1:8000](http://127.0.0.1:8000)
* [http://127.0.0.1:8000/docs](http://127.0.0.1:8000/docs)

Confirm it shows your **FDIF API** home message — and we can move to the **Excel ingestion** step right after that.
